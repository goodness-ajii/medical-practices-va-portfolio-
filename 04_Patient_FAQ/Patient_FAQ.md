## 1. How to Use This FAQ

This FAQ is designed for two audiences:

- **The Virtual Receptionist** — as a quick-reference answer guide during live calls.
- **The patient** — printable / shareable as a one-page handout or website resource.

**Rules of thumb when using these answers on a call:**
- **Never diagnose.** Phrases like "your provider will assess this at consultation" keep you in scope.
- **Never quote final pricing** for medical treatments — give ranges and defer to the provider.
- **Always offer the next step** (book a consult, send a Client Portal link, take a message).
- **Verify identity** (name + DOB) before discussing anything specific to a patient's record.

## 5. Consultations

**Q: Why do I need a consultation first?**
A: A consultation lets your provider evaluate your concerns, review your medical history, and recommend the safest and most effective plan. It also gives you a chance to ask questions in a no-pressure environment.

**Q: What happens during a consultation?**

## 6. Botox & Neurotoxins

**Q: What is Botox?**
A: Botox is a neurotoxin that temporarily relaxes the muscles that cause expression lines (forehead lines, frown lines between the brows, and crow's feet around the eyes).

**Q: How long does Botox last?**
A: Typically 3–4 months. Some clients on a regular maintenance schedule see results last slightly longer over time.

**Q: When will I see results?**
A: Most clients begin to see results within 3–5 days, with full results at 10–14 days.

**Q: Does Botox hurt?**
A: Most clients describe it as a brief pinch. We can apply a cool compress beforehand for added comfort.

**Q: Is there downtime?**
A: No real downtime. You can return to your normal day immediately. We do ask you to avoid strenuous exercise, lying flat, and heat exposure for 4 hours.

**Q: Am I too young / too old for Botox?**
A: There's no perfect age — our providers treat clients in their 20s for prevention and clients well into their 70s for refresh. The right time is when it aligns with your goals and your provider approves.

**Q: What's the difference between Botox, Dysport, and Xeomin?**
A: All three are neurotoxins that work similarly. They differ slightly in how quickly they take effect and how they spread. Your provider will recommend the best option for your goals.

## 7. Dermal Fillers

**Q: What are dermal fillers?**
A: Fillers are gel-based products (most commonly hyaluronic acid) that restore volume, smooth lines, and enhance contours in areas like the lips, cheeks, and under-eyes.

**Q: Which filler do you use?**
A: We carry the Juvederm, Restylane, and RHA collections. Your provider will recommend the right one based on the area being treated and your desired result.

**Q: How long do fillers last?**
A: Lip filler typically lasts 6–12 months. Cheek and chin filler can last 12–18 months. Results vary by product and individual metabolism.

**Q: Will I look "done" or unnatural?**
A: Our providers specialize in natural-looking results. You can communicate exactly how subtle or noticeable you want your results to be during your consultation.

**Q: Is there downtime?**
A: Mild swelling and possible bruising for 3–7 days. Most clients return to normal activities the same day. We recommend avoiding major events for 1–2 weeks after.

**Q: Can fillers be reversed?**
A: Hyaluronic acid fillers can be dissolved with an enzyme called hyaluronidase if needed.

## 9. Microneedling

**Q: What is microneedling?**
A: Microneedling uses tiny needles to create controlled micro-channels in the skin, stimulating natural collagen production. It improves texture, fine lines, scarring, and overall skin quality.

**Q: Does microneedling hurt?**
A: We apply a numbing cream for 20–30 minutes before treatment, so most clients feel only mild pressure or a vibrating sensation.

**Q: How many sessions do I need?**
A: Most clients see best results from a series of 3–4 sessions spaced 4–6 weeks apart.

**Q: How long is the downtime?**
A: Skin is typically pink and slightly sensitive for 24–48 hours. Most clients return to normal activities the next day with mineral-based makeup if desired.

**Q: What is microneedling with PRP?**
A: PRP (platelet-rich plasma) is drawn from your own blood and applied during microneedling to enhance healing and results. It's optional and discussed at consultation.

## 10. Laser Hair Removal

**Q: How does laser hair removal work?**
A: Laser energy targets the pigment in hair follicles to reduce future hair growth. Results are progressive over a series of sessions.

**Q: How many sessions do I need?**
A: Most clients need 6–8 sessions spaced 4–8 weeks apart for optimal results.

**Q: Does it hurt?**
A: Most clients describe it as a quick warm snap. Our laser has a built-in cooling system for comfort.

**Q: Can I get laser hair removal in the summer?**
A: Yes, but the treated area must be shielded from sun exposure with SPF 30+. Tan or sunburned skin cannot be safely treated.

**Q: Are there areas you can't treat?**
A: We do not treat directly over tattoos. Around the eyes is also restricted. Your provider will review safe treatment areas at consultation.

## 12. Pre-Treatment Guidance

**Q: What should I do before Botox or filler?**
A: Avoid the following for 24–48 hours before your appointment:
- Alcohol
- Aspirin, ibuprofen, naproxen, and other NSAIDs (unless medically required)
- Fish oil, vitamin E, garlic, and ginkgo supplements
- High-intensity workouts the morning of

These can increase bruising risk.

**Q: Can I get treatment if I'm sick?**
A: Please reschedule if you have a cold, flu, fever, active cold sore, or skin infection at the treatment area.

**Q: Should I come with makeup on?**
A: It's fine to arrive with makeup, but we'll cleanse the treatment area before any procedure.

**Q: Can I get treatment if I'm pregnant or breastfeeding?**
A: We do not offer injectables or laser treatments during pregnancy or breastfeeding. Some skincare facials may still be safe — please mention this when booking so we can guide you.

## 13. Post-Treatment Care

**Q: What should I avoid after Botox?**
A: For the first 4 hours: avoid lying flat, vigorous exercise, heat exposure (sauna, hot tub), and rubbing the treated area. You can resume normal activities the next day.

**Q: What should I avoid after filler?**
A: For 24–48 hours: avoid strenuous exercise, alcohol, sun exposure, and saunas. Sleep on your back if possible. Avoid dental work for 2 weeks.

**Q: When can I wear makeup after microneedling?**
A: We recommend waiting 24 hours, then using only mineral-based makeup until your skin has fully recovered.

**Q: How long until I see final results?**
A: Botox: 10–14 days. Filler: 1–2 weeks once swelling resolves. HydraFacial: immediate. Microneedling: progressive over 4–6 weeks.

**Q: When should I book my next appointment?**
A: Most clients rebook before they leave. We'll help you plan your next visit so your results stay consistent.

## 14. Safety, Side Effects & When to Call

**Q: What are common side effects?**
A: Mild redness, swelling, and possible bruising at the injection or treatment site are normal and typically resolve within a few days.

**Q: When should I call the practice?**
A: Please call us **immediately** if you experience:
- Severe or worsening pain
- Spreading redness or signs of infection (warmth, pus, fever)
- Skin that turns white, blue, gray, or feels cold after filler (possible vascular concern — call urgently)
- Difficulty breathing, swallowing, or vision changes
- Significant or asymmetric swelling
- Any reaction that feels wrong or alarming to you

**If symptoms are severe** (breathing difficulty, vision changes, vascular signs), call **911 or go to your nearest emergency room** while also calling our office.

**Q: Is it normal to bruise?**
A: Some bruising is possible, especially with filler. Arnica gel and avoiding blood-thinning supplements before treatment can help reduce bruising.

**Q: Will the treatment look uneven at first?**
A: Mild asymmetry from swelling is common in the first few days. Final results are typically assessed at 2 weeks.

## 17. Cancellations, Late Arrivals & No-Shows

A: Yes. You can request cancellations or reschedules through the Client Portal up to 24 hours before your appointment.
