## Section 1: Common MedSpa Services

Aesthetics practices typically offer a range of non-surgical treatments. The following are among the most commonly requested services in the industry.

### 1.1 Injectables (Botulinum Toxin & Dermal Fillers)

#### Botox / Dysport / Xeomin (Neurotoxin Injectables)

**What it is:** Botulinum toxin that works by blocking nerve signals to facial muscles, reducing their ability to contract and form wrinkles.

**Medical Information**

| Detail | Information |
|---|---|
| Most common treatment areas | Forehead lines, glabellar region (between eyebrows), crow's feet around eyes |
| How it works | Gradually relaxes dynamic wrinkles caused by facial expression |
| Results timeline | Initial results visible 3–7 days; full results appear by day 14 |
| Duration | Results typically last 3–4 months; maintenance treatments every 3–4 months |
| Typical cost range | $12–$15 per unit; average treatment uses 20–40 units per area |
| Pre-care considerations | Avoid blood thinners and alcohol 24 hours before; avoid other facial treatments for 1 week prior |
| Post-care requirements | Avoid strenuous exercise for 24 hours; do not lie down for 3–4 hours after; avoid touching treated areas; use SPF 30+ for 1–2 weeks |
| Downtime | None; patients can return to normal activities immediately |
| Possible side effects | Mild bruising, minimal swelling, temporary uneven results during the first week |

**Common Patient Questions & Professional Responses**

| Patient Question | Professional Response |
|---|---|
| "Will I look frozen?" | Modern techniques aim for natural results. Providers use conservative dosing to maintain facial expression while smoothing wrinkles. |
| "How long does it actually take to work?" | You will notice initial changes around day 3–5, but full results develop by week 2. Patience is important. |
| "Is it permanent?" | No, it is completely reversible. Results fade naturally over 3–4 months as your body metabolizes the product. |
| "What's the difference between Botox and Dysport?" | Both are neurotoxins that work the same way. Dysport may diffuse slightly more; Botox is more localized. Your provider can recommend based on your goals. |
| "Is it safe?" | Botox has been used in aesthetics for over 20 years with an excellent safety profile. It is administered by licensed medical professionals. |

#### Dermal Fillers (Hyaluronic Acid & Others)

**What it is:** Injectable gels that add volume to areas where the skin has lost fullness or collagen due to aging.

**Medical Information**

| Detail | Information |
|---|---|
| Most common treatment areas | Lips, cheeks, under-eye hollows, nasolabial folds, chin definition |
| How it works | Adds volume directly to the treatment area; also stimulates the body's own collagen production |
| Results | Visible immediately; slight swelling for first 24–48 hours |
| Duration | Typically 6–12 months, depending on the product used and patient metabolism |
| Typical cost range | $400–$800+ per syringe; most treatments use 1–2 syringes |
| Pre-care | Avoid blood thinners for 24 hours before |
| Post-care | Avoid strenuous exercise for 24 hours; ice for first 24 hours if swelling; avoid touching treated areas; use SPF 30+ |
| Downtime | Minimal; some swelling and possible bruising for 24–48 hours |
| Possible side effects | Temporary swelling, bruising, asymmetry |

**Common Patient Questions & Professional Responses**

| Patient Question | Professional Response |
|---|---|
| "Will it look fake/puffy?" | Experienced providers use a conservative approach, building volume gradually. Results look most natural once initial swelling subsides. |
| "What if I hate it?" | Hyaluronic acid fillers can be adjusted or partially dissolved if needed. |
| "How long do I need to wait to see final results?" | Wait 1–2 weeks for swelling to completely subside before judging final results. |
| "Can I combine this with other treatments?" | Yes, fillers and Botox are often done together in the same appointment. |

### 1.2 Laser & Light Therapies

#### Laser Hair Removal

**What it is:** Uses concentrated light energy to target and permanently reduce hair growth by destroying hair follicles at the root.

**Medical Information**

| Detail | Information |
|---|---|
| How it works | Laser energy is absorbed by melanin (pigment) in the hair, destroying the follicle |
| Treatment areas | Legs, underarms, bikini area, face, back, chest |
| Sessions needed | Typically 6–8 sessions for significant hair reduction |
| Spacing between sessions | 4–6 weeks |
| Results timeline | Progressive reduction with each session |
| Duration of results | Long-lasting; many patients achieve permanent or near-permanent reduction |
| Typical cost | Series of 6–8 treatments (often discounted packages available) |
| Pre-care | Avoid sun exposure for 1 week; avoid plucking or waxing for 1 week before |
| Post-care | Avoid sun exposure for 1–2 weeks; use SPF 30+; avoid hot water and exercise for 24 hours |
| Downtime | None |
| Possible side effects | Temporary redness, mild swelling, slight sensitivity |

**Common Patient Questions & Professional Responses**

| Patient Question | Professional Response |
|---|---|
| "Will it hurt?" | Most patients describe it as a light snapping sensation. Numbing cream can be applied beforehand if sensitive. |
| "Does it work on dark skin?" | Modern laser technology has been adapted for all skin types. |
| "Why do I need multiple sessions?" | Hair grows in cycles. Lasers only work on actively growing hair. |
| "Is it really permanent?" | Most patients see permanent or near-permanent reduction. |

#### Laser Skin Resurfacing

**What it is:** Uses laser energy to remove the outer layers of damaged skin and stimulate collagen remodeling.

| Detail | Information |
|---|---|
| Treatments | CO2 laser or fractional laser |
| What it treats | Sun damage, age spots, uneven skin tone, fine lines, acne scars, skin texture |
| Results | Progressive improvement over 3–6 months |
| Duration | Long-lasting |
| Downtime | 5–10 days for ablative; 2–5 days for fractional |
| Cost | Varies widely based on treatment type and area |
| Pre-care | Avoid sun for 2 weeks; avoid retinol for 1 week |
| Post-care | Strict sun protection, use healing ointment, avoid makeup for several days |
| Possible side effects | Redness, peeling, temporary sensitivity |

### 1.3 Chemical Peels

**What it is:** Application of chemical solutions to the skin that safely remove the outer damaged layers, promoting cell turnover and collagen stimulation.

| Detail | Information |
|---|---|
| Types by depth | Superficial (light, minimal downtime) to Deep (significant downtime) |
| What they treat | Fine lines, acne, uneven skin tone, sun damage, texture |
| Results | Smoother, clearer, more radiant skin |
| Typical regimen | Series of 4–6 peels spaced 2–4 weeks apart |
| Downtime | Superficial (1–3 days), Medium (5–7 days), Deep (1–2 weeks) |
| Cost | $100–$300+ per peel depending on depth |
| Pre-care | Avoid sun exposure; possibly stop retinol for 1 week |
| Post-care | Gentle skin care, SPF 50+, avoid sun, do not pick at peeling skin, moisturize well |
| Possible side effects | Redness, peeling, dryness, sensitivity (expected and temporary) |

### 1.4 Microneedling / RF Microneedling

**What it is:** Minimally invasive procedure using tiny sterile needles to create controlled micro-injuries in the skin, triggering collagen production.

| Detail | Information |
|---|---|
| What it treats | Acne scars, surgical scars, fine lines, skin texture, large pores, stretch marks |
| Sessions needed | 3–6 treatments for optimal results |
| Spacing | 4–6 weeks between treatments |
| Results timeline | Progressive; best results visible 3 months after series |
| Downtime | 24–48 hours of redness and sensitivity |
| Cost | $200–$400+ per treatment |
| Pre-care | Avoid sun; avoid vitamin A/retinol for 1 week |
| Post-care | Gentle cleansing, soothing serums, SPF 50+, avoid makeup for 24 hours |
| Possible side effects | Mild redness, slight sensitivity, minimal bruising possible |

### 1.5 Facials & Professional Skincare Treatments

Aesthetics practices typically offer professional facials that are more advanced than standard spa facials.

**Common types:**

- **HydraFacial:** Vacuum extraction combined with serums
- **Chemical facials:** Combines superficial chemical peel with serums and extraction
- **Oxygen facials:** Infuses serums into skin using pressurized oxygen
- **Customized facials:** Designed for specific skin concerns
- **Medical-grade facials:** Using professional-strength products

**Pricing and packages:** Facials typically cost $150–$300 per treatment. Many practices offer package deals or monthly membership bundles.

## Section 2: Typical Patient Journey

Understanding how patients move through the aesthetic practice journey helps a Virtual Receptionist anticipate questions, schedule appropriately, and follow up effectively.

### Stage 1: Awareness & Initial Inquiry (First Phone Call)

**How patients find the practice:**

- Social media advertising (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok)
- Google search
- Referral from friend or family
- Online reviews
- Influencer recommendations
- Local event sponsorships

**What patients typically ask on first call:**

- "Do you offer [specific service]?"
- "How much does [treatment] cost?"
- "How long do results last?"
- "When can I get in?"
- "Is there a consultation appointment?"
- "Do you take my insurance?"
- "What can I expect?"

**VA role at this stage:**
You will answer questions professionally and accurately, provide brief service descriptions, explain realistic expectations, discuss cost and payment options, and schedule appropriately.

### Stage 2: Consultation (If Scheduled)

Some practices schedule a separate consultation appointment; others combine it with treatment. Understanding your specific practice's protocol is essential.

**If separate consultation:**

- Patient meets with provider for 30–45 minutes
- Provider asks about patient goals and medical history
- Provider performs skin/facial analysis
- Provider shows before/after photos
- Provider recommends specific treatment plan and cost
- Patient decides to proceed or schedule for another time

### Stage 3: Treatment Appointment

**Appointment day flow:**

- Patient arrives 10–15 minutes early
- Receptionist checks in patient and verifies insurance/payment
- Patient completes/reviews consent forms
- Patient is brought to treatment room
- Treatment is performed
- Provider reviews post-care instructions
- Patient checks out and schedules follow-up if applicable

### Stage 4: Post-Treatment Follow-Up (Days 1–28)

**What practices typically do:**

- Send post-care reminder text/email same evening as treatment
- Call/text at 24 hours to check in
- Send 2-week check-in reminder for injectables
- Ask for feedback on results
- Offer adjustment/touch-up if needed

**VA role at this stage:**
You are the first point of contact for concerns. Field post-treatment calls, reassure on normal side effects, recognize RED FLAGS, document conversations, and schedule follow-ups.

### Stage 5: Maintenance & Retention (Month 2+)

**What practices typically do:**

- Send reminders for maintenance appointments
- Offer package deals
- Introduce membership programs
- Cross-sell related services
- Offer referral incentives
- Run seasonal promotions

## Section 3: Key MedSpa Industry Concepts

Understanding these concepts helps you speak confidently and make appropriate decisions in patient interactions.

### Concept 1: Pre-Care & Post-Care Compliance

**Pre-care (What patients do BEFORE treatment):**

- Avoid blood-thinning medications for 24–48 hours before
- Avoid alcohol for 24 hours before
- Avoid vitamin E supplements for 24 hours before
- Avoid strenuous exercise for 24 hours before
- Avoid other facial treatments for 1–2 weeks before
- Avoid sun exposure for 1–2 weeks before
- Possibly avoid retinol or vitamin A products for 3–5 days before

**Post-care (What patients do AFTER treatment):**

- Avoid strenuous exercise for 24–48 hours
- Avoid extreme temperatures for 24–48 hours
- Avoid touching or rubbing treated areas for 24–48 hours
- Do not apply makeup for 24 hours
- Use ice for first 24 hours if swelling
- Use gentle, fragrance-free cleanser
- Use SPF 30+ sunscreen daily
- Stay hydrated
- Avoid alcohol for 24–48 hours

**Why it matters:** Compliance significantly impacts patient outcomes and satisfaction. As VA, you will reinforce these instructions during phone calls and flag non-compliance for provider review.

### Concept 2: Packages & Membership Models

**Package (Series Deal):**
A patient purchases multiple treatments upfront at a discounted price. This increases patient commitment and improves compliance.

**Membership (Monthly Subscription):**
A patient pays a monthly fee and receives certain benefits. This creates predictable revenue and improves patient loyalty.

**VA role:**
Explain package options to inquiring patients, help patients choose what fits their budget, track package usage, and manage membership billing.

### Concept 3: Results Timeline & Realistic Expectations

**Treatment-specific timelines for Injectables (Botox, Filler):**

| Day Range | What to Expect |
|---|---|
| Days 1–3 | Minimal visible change |
| Days 4–7 | Initial results appearing |
| Days 14–21 | Full results visible |
| Weeks 3–4 | Peak results |
| Month 3 | Results begin to fade slightly |

**VA responsibility:** When a patient calls early saying results are not visible yet, reassure them with the timeline and provide post-care reminders.

### Concept 4: Red Flags (When to Escalate Immediately to Provider)

**Signs of infection:**

- Increasing warmth/heat in the area
- Pus or unusual drainage
- Fever
- Swelling that worsens after day 3
- Red streaking

**Signs of allergic reaction:**

- Hives or rash
- Difficulty breathing
- Swelling of lips, mouth, or throat
- Severe facial swelling

**Other concerns:**
Blanching or pain at injection site, drooping eyelid, patient emotional distress.

**VA Protocol:**

> IF PATIENT REPORTS RED FLAGS: Listen, take seriously, say "I am going to get the provider on the phone with you right now," immediately contact provider, document verbatim, and follow up as directed.
>
> If patient reports severe allergic reaction or difficulty breathing: **Call 911 first.**
