## Why a Voice Demo Matters

For a Virtual Receptionist role, the **phone is the product**. Most VA applicants describe their phone manner — almost none demonstrate it. A 60–90 second voice demo immediately separates you from 95% of applicants, because clients can *hear* whether you sound:

- Warm
- Calm
- Clear
- Professional
- Genuinely friendly

Even a phone-recorded demo is fine. What matters is the **delivery**, not studio quality.

## Part 1: Pre-Recording Preparation

### Equipment Checklist

| Item | Minimum Acceptable | Recommended |
|---|---|---|
| Microphone | Smartphone built-in mic | USB mic (e.g., Blue Yeti Nano) or quality headset mic |
| Recording app | Phone Voice Memos / Vocaroo (free, web-based) | Audacity (free desktop app) |
| Environment | Closed quiet room | Closet with hanging clothes (best home sound) |
| Background noise | None — turn off fans, AC, notifications | Same |
| Time of day | When the house is quietest | Same |

**Free tools:**
- 🎙️ **Vocaroo** (vocaroo.com) — record straight in the browser, instant shareable link
- 🎙️ **Audacity** (audacityteam.org) — free desktop app for cleaner edits
- 🎙️ **iPhone Voice Memos** or **Android Recorder** — built-in, totally fine for portfolio

### Vocal Warm-Up (5 minutes before recording)

1. Drink room-temperature water (avoid cold or dairy — they coat the throat).
2. Hum from low to high pitch for 30 seconds.
3. Read this tongue-twister 3 times: *"Red leather, yellow leather, red leather, yellow leather."*
4. Smile — actually smile. Your voice will sound warmer.
5. Stand up if possible — you'll project better than sitting.
6. Breathe from your diaphragm (belly), not your chest.

### The 5 Delivery Rules

| Rule | What It Means |
|---|---|
| 1. **Smile while speaking** | Your tone literally changes. Listeners can hear a smile. |
| 2. **Slow down** | Speak about 20% slower than your conversational pace. |
| 3. **Pause at commas** | Don't rush punctuation. Pauses signal calm and confidence. |
| 4. **Lower your voice slightly** | A slightly lower pitch sounds more authoritative and reassuring. |
| 5. **Pronounce final consonants** | "Today" not "todeh." Crisp endings = professional. |

## Part 2: The Demo Scripts

You'll record **three** short clips. Together they tell a complete story: greeting, scheduling, and reassurance under pressure.

### Clip 1 — New Patient Inquiry (45–60 seconds)

This shows your warm greeting, listening skills, and conversion ability.

> **(You):** Thank you for calling Glow Aesthetics, this is Goodness. How may I help you today?
>
> **(Pause — imagine the caller responds: "Hi, I'm interested in getting Botox.")**
>
> **(You):** Wonderful! Botox is one of our most requested treatments — it's great for softening fine lines and refreshing your overall look. May I ask what concerns you're hoping to address?
>
> **(Pause — caller says: "I just want to look fresher.")**
>
> **(You):** That's a beautiful goal, and absolutely something we can help with. We always begin with a complimentary consultation so the provider can assess your skin and recommend the right plan for you. I have an opening this Thursday at 2 PM with Nurse Jamie, or Friday morning at 10 AM. Which works better for you?
>
> **(Pause — caller says: "Thursday at 2 works great.")**
>
> **(You):** Perfect. I have you booked for Thursday at 2 PM with Jamie. I'll send your intake forms through our Client Portal so everything is ready before your visit. Is there anything else I can help you with today?
>
> **(Pause)**
>
> **(You):** Wonderful. Thank you so much for calling Glow Aesthetics — we look forward to seeing you Thursday. Have a beautiful day.

**Delivery notes:**
- Smile audibly on "Wonderful!" and "Have a beautiful day."
- Slow down on the date/time confirmation — clarity matters.
- The pause after "How may I help you today?" should be 2–3 seconds of silence on the recording.

---

### Clip 2 — Outbound Appointment Confirmation (30–40 seconds)

This shows your concise, friendly outbound call style.

> **(You):** Hi, may I speak with Jessica? *(Pause)*
>
> **(You):** Hi Jessica, this is Goodness from Glow Aesthetics. I'm calling to confirm your appointment with Nurse Jamie this Thursday, May 28th, at 1 PM. Will you still be able to make it?
>
> **(Pause)**
>
> **(You):** Wonderful, thank you so much for confirming. Just a quick reminder — please avoid alcohol and any blood thinners like ibuprofen for 24 hours before your appointment to minimize bruising. If anything changes, you can reach us at (305) 555-0140. We're looking forward to seeing you Thursday. Have a beautiful day!

**Delivery notes:**
- Sound brisk but warm — outbound confirmations should be efficient.
- The pre-care reminder is a credibility moment. Speak it clearly.
- End with energy — patients hang up remembering the last 3 seconds.

---

### Clip 3 — Calm Handling of a Concerned Patient (45–60 seconds)

This is the **most important clip** for medspa work. It shows you can handle a worried post-treatment caller with composure — exactly what a clinic needs.

> **(You):** Thank you for calling Glow Aesthetics, this is Goodness. How may I help you today?
>
> **(Pause — imagine caller says, anxiously: "Hi, I had filler yesterday and my lips are really swollen and I'm worried.")**
>
> **(You):** *(calm, warm)* I completely understand, and I'm so glad you called us. Some swelling in the first 24 to 48 hours is normal and expected — but I want to make sure you're comfortable, so I'm going to connect you with our nurse right away. Before I do, may I confirm your full name and date of birth, please?
>
> **(Pause)**
>
> **(You):** Thank you, Mrs. Lopez. Stay right on the line for me — I'm going to message Nurse Jamie now and have her call you back within fifteen minutes. If anything changes before then — if you have any difficulty breathing, vision changes, or if your skin feels cold or looks pale — please call 911 or go to your nearest emergency room. But for what you're describing right now, this is something Jamie will be able to help you with directly. Does that sound okay?
>
> **(Pause)**
>
> **(You):** Wonderful. You're going to be just fine. Hang tight, Mrs. Lopez — Jamie will call you within fifteen minutes. Thank you for trusting us, and we'll talk soon.

**Delivery notes:**
- This is the key clip. Slow your pace 30%.
- Drop your pitch slightly to sound calming.
- Never say *"Don't worry"* — say *"You're going to be just fine"* or *"I completely understand."*
- The 911 instruction is the credibility moment — say it clearly without rushing.

## Part 3: Recording Workflow

### Step-by-step

1. **Read each script out loud 3 times** before recording — once slow, once at pace, once for delivery.
2. **Record each clip separately** — not all in one take.
3. **Do at least 3 takes per clip.** Pick the best one.
4. **Listen back** with headphones. If you cringe, re-record. If you'd hire yourself, save it.
5. **Save with clear filenames:**
   - `Goodness_Ajii_Demo_01_NewPatient.mp3`
   - `Goodness_Ajii_Demo_02_Confirmation.mp3`
   - `Goodness_Ajii_Demo_03_Concerned.mp3`
6. **Upload** to Google Drive (link sharing on, "Anyone with link can view") OR Vocaroo for instant URLs.

### What to listen for on playback

| Issue | Fix |
|---|---|
| Sounds rushed | Pause more at commas; slow down |
| Sounds flat/monotone | Smile while speaking; vary pitch slightly |
| Background noise | Re-record in a quieter space or use a closet |
| Mouth clicks / breath sounds | Sip water; pause briefly before recording |
| "Um," "uh," "like" filler words | Re-record — these undermine professionalism |
| Voice trails off at end of sentences | Project through to the period; don't fade |

## Part 4: Common Mistakes to Avoid

| ❌ Mistake | Why It Hurts | ✅ Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Recording in a hard-walled room | Echoey, unprofessional | Record in a closet or carpeted room |
| Eating/drinking just before recording | Mouth noise on mic | Wait 30 minutes after eating |
| Reading the script too obviously | Sounds robotic | Practice until the words feel natural |
| Speaking too fast | Sounds anxious | Pace yourself; pause at punctuation |
| Sitting hunched over | Restricts breathing | Stand or sit upright |
| Overly casual ("hey," "yeah," "no worries") | Sounds unprofessional | Stick to the script's professional language |
| Using a non-business voice ("Hello??") | Sounds unprepared | Use the practice greeting every time |
| Skipping the smile | Tone falls flat | Smile through every clip |

## Part 7: Bonus Tips for Standing Out

1. **Re-record every 6 months.** Your voice and confidence will improve.
2. **Customize a clip per practice.** When applying to a specific clinic, re-record Clip 1 using their actual practice name. It takes 5 minutes and clients notice immediately.
3. **Add a 15-second "intro clip"** that says: *"Hi, I'm Goodness, a Registered Nurse and Certified Medical Virtual Assistant. Here are three short samples of how I'd handle calls for your medspa. Thank you for listening."*
4. **Mention the demo in your email signature** when applying: *"🎧 Hear my phone demo: [link]"*
