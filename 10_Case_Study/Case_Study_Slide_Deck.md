## 3. The Challenge

A growing aesthetics practice needs a reliable virtual receptionist to:

- Handle 100+ inbound calls per week during clinic hours
- Convert new inquiries into booked consultations
- Manage scheduling, reschedules, and cancellations
- Document every interaction professionally
- Recognize urgent post-treatment concerns and escalate appropriately
- Maintain HIPAA compliance from a remote workspace

## 5. Standard Operating Procedure

A 15-section SOP defines exactly how every call is handled:

- Answer within 3 rings using a signature warm greeting
- Triage: new patient, existing patient, urgent, vendor, wrong number
- Identity verification (name + DOB) before any PHI discussion
- HIPAA-compliant voicemail templates
- Escalation matrix specifying who to contact, by when, for what
- End-of-shift handoff protocol

---

## 6. Call Scripts

Eight ready-to-use scripts covering:

- New patient inquiry
- Consultation scheduling
- Post-treatment follow-up
- Reminder and rebooking
- Voicemail return
- HIPAA-compliant outbound voicemail
- Objection handling library
- Urgent escalation

Sample excerpt:

> **Caller:** "Do you offer Botox?"
>
> **Receptionist:** "Yes, we do. Botox is one of our most requested treatments for softening fine lines. May I ask what concerns you're hoping to address so I can point you to the best option?"

## 7. Patient FAQ

Seventy-plus questions covering:

- Booking and pricing
- Botox, fillers, HydraFacial, microneedling, laser hair removal
- Pre-care and post-care guidance
- Memberships and packages
- Privacy and HIPAA
- Cancellations and late arrivals

Every answer is written to defer to the provider on clinical decisions and never diagnose — protecting both the patient and the practice.

## 9. HIPAA Compliance

A 100-point self-audit covering:

- Workspace security (private locked room, headset, no PHI on desk)
- Device security (encryption, MFA, auto-lock, VPN)
- Communication security (only BAA-covered tools)
- Voicemail rules (callback only — no PHI)
- Incident response protocol
- Annual training renewal

> Voicemails contain only: first name, practice name, callback number. Never treatment details, never appointment specifics.

---

## 10. Strategic Reporting

Weekly performance reports include:

- Call volume and KPI scorecard
- Booking conversion rates
- Repeated patient questions worth website or FAQ updates
- Friction patterns (overbooked slots, voicemail spikes)
- Concrete recommendations for practice growth

Sample insight:

> *"8 callers asked about a Botox + lip filler combo this week. Worth packaging as a 'Refresh Combo Consult' to capture demand."*
