## 1. Purpose & How to Use This Document

This document equips the Virtual Receptionist with **practical, word-for-word call scripts** for the most common patient interactions at a medspa practice. Each section is designed to be:

- **Word-for-word usable** during a live call
- **Frameworks-driven** so the VA can adapt to unexpected turns
- **Objection-ready** with prepared responses to the most common patient hesitations
- **Escalation-aware** to protect both the patient and the practice

**How to use:**
- Print or pin the *Quick Reference Script Flow* (Section 12) at your workstation.
- Read each script aloud at least 3 times before your first shift — natural delivery matters more than memorization.
- Update the script library quarterly based on real patient questions you encounter.

## 2. Universal Call Standards

These standards apply to **every** call, inbound or outbound.

| Standard | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Greeting | "Thank you for calling Glow Aesthetics, this is [Name]. How may I help you today?" |
| Tone | Warm, calm, unhurried, with a "smiling voice" |
| Pace | Slower than conversational; clear enunciation |
| Identity verification | Verify name + DOB before discussing any health information |
| Call documentation | Every call logged within 5 minutes of ending |
| Sign-off | Confirm next steps + thank the caller by name |

## 3. Initial Inquiry Calls

### 3.1 Scenario
A potential client calls asking about a service — most commonly Botox, fillers, or a facial — and is gathering information.

### 3.2 Word-for-Word Script

**Front Desk:** Thank you for calling Glow Aesthetics, this is [Your Name]. How may I help you today?

**Caller:** Do you offer Botox?

**Front Desk:** Yes, we do. Botox is one of our most requested treatments for softening fine lines and expression wrinkles. May I ask what concerns you're hoping to address so I can point you to the best option?

**Caller:** I want to look fresher.

**Front Desk:** That's a great goal. We can absolutely help with that. We usually begin with a consultation so the provider can evaluate your concerns and recommend the right treatment plan. Would you like me to help you schedule a medspa consultation?

**Caller:** Yes.

**Front Desk:** Wonderful. I can help with that now. We'll also send your intake forms through the Client Portal so everything is ready before your visit.

### 3.3 Framework / Talking Points

- Greet warmly and professionally.
- Confirm the service requested.
- Ask one or two goal-based questions.
- Briefly explain the purpose of a consultation.
- Offer the next step: book consult, transfer, or take a message.
- Keep the tone calm, confident, and helpful.

### 3.4 Objection Handling

| Objection | Suggested Response |
|---|---|
| "I'm just shopping around." | "That's completely fine. A consultation is the best way to get personalized recommendations before making a decision." |
| "I don't want anything too dramatic." | "Many patients want a natural look. Your provider can keep results subtle and refreshed." |
| "How much does it cost?" | "Pricing depends on your treatment plan, so the best next step is a consultation where the provider can review options with you." |

### 3.5 Red Flags to Escalate

- Severe swelling
- Difficulty breathing
- Vision changes
- Severe pain
- Signs of allergic reaction
- A caller describing a complication after treatment

### 3.6 SimplePractice Terminology In Use

- Client
- Inquiry
- Consultation
- Appointment Request
- Client Portal
- Progress Note

## 4. Consultation Scheduling

### 4.1 Scenario
The caller is ready to book a consultation.

### 4.2 Word-for-Word Script

**Front Desk:** I'd be happy to schedule your consultation. We have availability on [day/date] at [time]. Would either of those work for you?

**Caller:** Yes, that works.

**Front Desk:** Perfect. I'll get that scheduled for you now. We'll send a confirmation and any intake forms to complete before your visit.

**Caller:** Great.

**Front Desk:** If you have any questions before then, you can reply through the Client Portal or give us a call.

### 4.3 Framework / Talking Points

- Offer two appointment options.
- Confirm the service and appointment length.
- Explain what the client should expect next.
- Mention intake forms and confirmation.
- End with reassurance and clear next steps.

### 4.4 Objection Handling

| Objection | Suggested Response |
|---|---|
| "Can I just come in without a consult?" | "For the best results and safest treatment plan, we recommend a consultation first." |
| "I'm not sure about the price." | "Your provider will review options and pricing during the consultation, so you can make an informed decision." |

### 4.5 Red Flags to Escalate

- Pregnancy or breastfeeding
- Serious medical conditions
- Requests outside clinic policy
- Unclear or conflicting medical history

### 4.6 SimplePractice Terminology In Use

- Appointment Request
- Availability
- Intake Forms
- Client Portal reminders

## 5. Post-Treatment Follow-Up Calls

### 5.1 Scenario
The office is following up after a medspa procedure (typically 24–72 hours post-treatment).

### 5.2 Word-for-Word Script

**Front Desk:** Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] calling from Glow Aesthetics. I'm checking in to see how you're feeling after your treatment.

**Client:** I'm doing okay.

**Front Desk:** That's good to hear. Mild redness or swelling can be normal, but if you notice anything unusual, please let us know right away.

**Front Desk:** I also wanted to remind you of your aftercare instructions and see whether you'd like help scheduling your follow-up appointment.

### 5.3 Framework / Talking Points

- Check comfort and overall well-being.
- Review expected side effects.
- Reinforce aftercare compliance.
- Offer assistance with follow-up scheduling.
- Keep the tone caring and reassuring.

### 5.4 Objection Handling

| Objection | Suggested Response |
|---|---|
| "I don't think I need follow-up." | "That's okay. We still recommend a check-in so we can monitor your results and plan maintenance if needed." |
| "I don't want to talk right now." | "Of course — I'll just leave a quick note that you're doing well. Please reach out anytime through the Client Portal if anything changes." |

### 5.5 Red Flags to Escalate

- Severe pain
- Worsening redness
- Fever
- Infection signs
- Unusual swelling
- Any issue involving vision or breathing

### 5.6 SimplePractice Terminology In Use

- Document the call in the Client record
- Add a Progress Note or communication note if that is part of the workflow

## 6. Reminder & Rebooking Calls

### 6.1 Scenario
The office calls to remind a client of an appointment or to rebook a future visit.

### 6.2 Word-for-Word Script

**Front Desk:** Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Glow Aesthetics. I'm calling to remind you about your upcoming appointment on [date/time].

**Client:** Thank you.

**Front Desk:** You're welcome. If you need to reschedule, I'd be happy to help you find another time that works better.

**Front Desk:** Also, if you'd like, we can help you plan your next visit so you stay on track with your treatment goals.

### 6.3 Framework / Talking Points

- Remind, confirm, and reduce no-shows.
- Offer easy rescheduling.
- Encourage rebooking before the call ends.
- Keep the conversation simple and efficient.

### 6.4 Objection Handling

| Objection | Suggested Response |
|---|---|
| "I forgot about it." | "No problem, I can help you reschedule right now." |
| "I'm too busy." | "We have a few alternate times available that may be more convenient." |
| "I'm not sure if I want to keep coming." | "I understand. Would you like me to leave a note for the provider so they can check in with you about your goals?" |

### 6.5 Red Flags to Escalate

- Client reports illness
- Recent medical changes
- Reaction to a previous treatment
- Any concern that may require provider review

### 6.6 SimplePractice Terminology In Use

- Appointment
- Reschedule
- Availability
- Reminder
- Client Portal

## 7. Voicemail Return Calls

### 7.1 Scenario
A caller left a voicemail outside of hours or while you were on another call. You are returning the call.

### 7.2 Word-for-Word Script (Live Answer)

**Front Desk:** Hi, may I speak with [Caller's First Name]? This is [Your Name] returning your call from Glow Aesthetics.

**Client:** Yes, this is [Name].

**Front Desk:** Thank you for calling earlier — I wanted to make sure we got back to you promptly. How can I help you today?

### 7.3 Framework / Talking Points

- Identify yourself and the practice clearly.
- Avoid disclosing why they called until you've confirmed identity.
- Verify name + DOB before discussing any health information.
- Document the return call attempt and outcome.

### 7.4 Voicemail Return Cadence

| Attempt | Timing | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | Within 30 min during clinic hours | Live call + voicemail if no answer |
| 2nd | Same day or next morning | Live call + voicemail |
| 3rd | Within 48 hours total | Live call; if no answer, send Client Portal message |

## 8. Outbound Voicemails (HIPAA-Compliant)

When the caller doesn't pick up, leave a **brief, generic** voicemail. **Never** disclose treatment details, appointment specifics, or any PHI in a voicemail.

### 8.1 Voicemail Script — General

> "Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from Glow Aesthetics. Please give us a call back at (305) 555-0140 at your convenience. Thank you and have a beautiful day."

### 8.2 Voicemail Script — Appointment Confirmation

> "Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from Glow Aesthetics. I'm calling to confirm your upcoming appointment. Please call us back at (305) 555-0140 to confirm. Thank you."

### 8.3 Voicemail Script — Returning a Patient's Call

> "Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] returning your call from Glow Aesthetics. Please give us a call back at (305) 555-0140. Thank you and have a wonderful day."

### 8.4 What NOT to Say in a Voicemail

- ❌ Specific treatments ("your Botox follow-up")
- ❌ Provider names tied to clinical detail ("Nurse Jamie wants to discuss your filler")
- ❌ Test or lab results
- ❌ Billing balances
- ❌ Anything that confirms the listener is a patient receiving a specific service

## 9. Objection Handling Library

A consolidated reference of the most common patient hesitations and how to respond.

### 9.1 Master Script for Hesitation

**Front Desk:** I understand price is an important factor. Many clients start with a consultation so they can choose the safest and most effective option for their budget and goals.

**Front Desk:** We want you to feel comfortable and informed before moving forward.

### 9.2 Framework

- **Acknowledge** the concern (don't argue or dismiss).
- **Reassure** without overexplaining.
- **Redirect** to consultation or provider recommendation.
- **Engage** — keep the client in conversation; don't let silence end the call.

### 9.3 Common Objections & Responses

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "It's too expensive." | "I understand. A consultation can help determine the most effective treatment plan within your budget." |
| "I need to think about it." | "Absolutely. I can send over the consultation details so you can review everything first." |
| "I'm not sure it will work." | "That's a fair concern. Your provider will explain expected results and what's realistic for your goals." |
| "I'm nervous about pain." | "Many patients feel that way at first. Your provider can explain comfort measures and what to expect." |
| "I had a bad experience elsewhere." | "I'm sorry to hear that. Your provider will take time to understand your concerns and create a plan you feel confident about." |
| "I'm not sure I'm a good candidate." | "That's exactly what the consultation is for — your provider will assess and let you know what's best for you." |

## 10. Red Flags & Escalation

### 10.1 When to Escalate Immediately

- Trouble breathing or swallowing
- Sudden vision changes
- Severe swelling after treatment
- Intense or worsening pain
- Possible allergic reaction
- Possible infection
- Skin discoloration, blanching, or coldness after filler (vascular concern)

### 10.2 How to Respond

1. **Stay calm.** Your tone sets the patient's tone.
2. **Do not give medical advice** beyond your role.
3. **Notify the provider immediately** via Slack `#urgent` and tag the on-duty nurse.
4. **Follow clinic policy** for escalation order: Nurse → Office Manager → Medical Director.
5. **Advise urgent care or 911** if instructed by protocol or if the patient cannot wait.
6. **Stay on the line** if symptoms are severe; otherwise reassure the patient that a nurse will call back within 15 minutes.
7. **Document immediately** in SimplePractice and the Call Log; flag the entry as URGENT.

### 10.3 Sample Escalation Language

**Front Desk:** I'm so sorry you're experiencing that. I'm going to notify the provider right away so they can review this with you as soon as possible. Please stay near your phone — a nurse will call you back within 15 minutes. If your symptoms get worse before then, please call 911 or go to your nearest emergency room.

## 11. SimplePractice Terminology Guide

Use these terms naturally in conversation to sound informed and professional.

| Term | Meaning / Use |
|---|---|
| **Client** | Patient (SimplePractice's preferred term) |
| **Inquiry** | A new lead reaching out before becoming a client |
| **Client Portal** | The patient-facing portal for forms, messages, and self-scheduling |
| **Appointment Request** | A pending, not-yet-confirmed booking |
| **Availability** | Provider's open time slots |
| **Intake Forms** | Pre-visit health and consent paperwork |
| **Progress Note** | Clinical note added after a visit |
| **Client Profile** | The patient's master record |
| **Calendar** | The scheduling view showing all bookings |
| **Reminder** | Automated SMS/email sent before an appointment |
| **Reschedule** | Move an existing appointment |
| **Follow-up** | A return visit to monitor results |

### 11.1 Example of Natural Use

**Front Desk:** I can send your Intake Forms through the Client Portal after I book your Appointment Request. We'll also add your visit to the Calendar and send a Reminder before your appointment.

This language fits the SimplePractice workflow where practices manage clients, intake forms, availability, reminders, telehealth, and scheduling from one system.

## 12. Quick Reference Script Flow

A printable one-page flow for your workstation.

### 12.1 Inbound Call Flow

```
1. Greet warmly
2. Identify the caller's need
3. Ask one or two clarifying questions
4. Offer consultation or appointment
5. Confirm contact details
6. Send Intake Forms via Client Portal
7. Close politely with clear next step
```

### 12.2 Follow-Up Call Flow

```
1. Introduce yourself and the practice
2. Check in on the client
3. Reinforce aftercare instructions
4. Offer scheduling help
5. Escalate if needed
6. Document the call in SimplePractice
```

### 12.3 Escalation Flow

```
1. Stay calm, listen actively
2. Capture: name, DOB, callback #, symptoms, treatment received
3. Slack #urgent + tag on-duty nurse
4. Reassure caller a nurse will call within 15 min
5. If severe → instruct 911 / ER
6. Document URGENT flag in Call Log + SimplePractice
```

## 13. Training Notes for Portfolio Use

- Answer calls professionally and on-brand

- Support scheduling, rescheduling, and rebooking
