**Prepared by:** Goodness Ajii, RN, Certified Medical Virtual Assistant
**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** May 2026

> **Disclaimer:** This SOP demonstrates how I structure a typical day as a Healthcare Virtual Assistant. The hours and tasks are illustrative — real workflows are adapted to the practice's specific software, hours, and team needs.

---

## Purpose

This document shows the practice owner exactly how I organize a typical workday. It demonstrates:

- **Time discipline** — how I batch tasks for efficiency
- **Coverage reliability** — what gets done and when
- **Communication rhythm** — how I sync with the in-clinic team
- **End-of-day accountability** — clean handoffs

The principle behind every block: **batch similar tasks together** to minimize context-switching and maximize accuracy.

---

## 1. Pre-Shift Checklist (7:30 – 8:00 AM)

Before I'm "live" for the day, I work through this 8-point checklist:

- [ ] Computer powered on, dual monitors positioned
- [ ] Headset tested for clear audio (in case of any patient calls)
- [ ] SimplePractice / EHR logged in
- [ ] Practice Gmail logged in (separate browser profile from personal)
- [ ] Slack open in `#admin`, `#scheduling`, and `#patient-messages` channels
- [ ] Today's appointment list pulled up
- [ ] Open follow-ups from yesterday's EOD report reviewed
- [ ] Coffee/water on the desk (real talk — focus depends on it)

## 2. Daily Hour-by-Hour Schedule

| Time | Block | Activities |
|---|---|---|
| **8:00 – 8:15 AM** | Morning sync | Read overnight Slack messages from team. Scan overnight patient portal messages. Review today's appointment list (count, types, providers). Note any urgent flags carried over from yesterday. |
| **8:15 – 9:30 AM** | Email triage block | Respond to all overnight patient emails. Forward clinical questions to providers. Send pre-written templates where appropriate. Flag urgent items for in-clinic team via Slack. |
| **9:30 – 11:00 AM** | Data entry block | Enter new patient intake forms into EHR. Update charts with results from yesterday. Reconcile any data discrepancies from prior day. Audit prior day's records for completeness. |
| **11:00 – 11:45 AM** | Confirmation calls + Insurance verification | 24-hour confirmation calls for tomorrow's patients (only those who didn't confirm via SMS). Verify insurance for tomorrow's new patients. Note any insurance issues for billing. |
| **11:45 AM – 12:30 PM** | Lunch break | (off-line; auto-responder on email) |
| **12:30 – 2:00 PM** | Forms + chart prep block | Send Client Portal intake forms to tomorrow's new patients. Prepare chart notes templates for tomorrow's complex cases. Process prescription refill requests forwarded by providers. Process specialist referral coordination. |
| **2:00 – 3:30 PM** | Patient outreach block | Follow-up emails to patients with overdue annual physicals. Test result delivery emails (portal-only — no PHI in email body). Recall list outreach (patients due for screenings). Birthday outreach (if practice does this). |
| **3:30 – 4:30 PM** | Scheduling block | Process any same-day booking requests. Reschedule any cancellations from earlier today. Confirm tomorrow's full schedule is locked in. Update tracker spreadsheet with all status changes. |
| **4:30 – 5:00 PM** | EOD reporting + handoff | Update Call/Email log with all entries from the day. Send EOD summary in Slack `#admin` channel. Flag any open items for tomorrow. Mark voicemails returned. System logout. |

## 3. The Communication Rhythm with the In-Clinic Team

Coordinating remotely with an in-clinic team requires structure. Here's how I sync:

| Channel | Use For | Response Expectation |
|---|---|---|
| **Slack `#admin`** | General updates, EOD reports, non-urgent questions | Within 30 min during clinic hours |
| **Slack `#scheduling`** | Schedule changes, same-day adds, cancellations | Within 15 min during clinic hours |
| **Slack `#urgent`** | Anything safety-critical or time-sensitive | Within 5 min — direct ping |
| **Email to Office Manager** | Documentation that needs a paper trail | Within 24 hours |
| **Phone to Office Manager** | Genuinely urgent situations only | Immediate |

## 4. Task Batching Principles

The reason this schedule works is **batching**: doing similar tasks back-to-back instead of context-switching.

| Task Type | Why Batched |
|---|---|
| **Email** (8:15–9:30) | Single mindset = faster, more consistent replies |
| **Data entry** (9:30–11:00) | Mental "tunnel" mode reduces typos |
| **Phone calls** (11:00–11:45) | Voice + tone warmed up consistently |
| **Patient outreach** (2:00–3:30) | Same templates, same headspace |

Batching saves an estimated **45–60 minutes per day** vs. handling tasks as they arrive.

Every Friday, I add 30 extra minutes for weekly housekeeping:

- [ ] Generate the **Weekly Performance Report** (separate document)
- [ ] Clean up shared Drive folders
- [ ] Email Office Manager with anything that needs their attention next week
- [ ] Submit my own weekly time log

## 7. What I Don't Do

To respect scope and stay HIPAA-compliant, here's what I **never** do, even if asked:

- Provide medical advice — clinical questions go to providers
- Refill controlled medications (Schedule II–IV) without provider authorization
- Diagnose or interpret labs for patients
- Send PHI via personal email or non-secured tools
- Discuss patient details with anyone outside the BAA-covered team
- Make scheduling exceptions outside posted policy without manager approval
- Process payments or refunds without sign-off
