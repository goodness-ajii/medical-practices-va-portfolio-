## Purpose

Email is the **primary written communication channel** for general medical VAs. Every email represents the practice's voice — and a single careless one can violate HIPAA.

This library provides **8 ready-to-use templates** I would deploy on day one of a healthcare VA engagement. Each template includes:

- **Use case** (when to send it)
- **Subject line** (clean, no PHI)
- **Body** (with merge fields)
- **HIPAA notes** (privacy considerations)
- **Tone notes** (delivery guidance)

---

## Universal Email Standards

These rules apply to **every** email I send on behalf of a practice:

| Standard | Why |
|---|---|
| **No PHI in subject lines** — ever | Subject lines are visible in notifications, lock screens, and spam filters |
| **Always greet by name** ("Hello Mrs. Johnson,") | Personalization signals care |
| **Plain English** — no jargon | Patients don't speak in medical abbreviations |
| **Action items in bold or numbered** | Increases response/compliance |
| **Sign off with full name + role + practice name** | Builds trust |
| **Reply within 2 business hours during clinic hours** | KPI standard |
| **Triple-check recipient** before sending — easy to email wrong patient | Preventable HIPAA breach |

## Template 1 — Appointment Confirmation (Sent at Booking)

**Use case:** Sent immediately after an appointment is booked.

**Subject:** Your appointment with Sunrise Family Medicine is confirmed

**Body:**
```
Hello [First Name],

Thank you for scheduling your visit with Sunrise Family Medicine & Wellness.

Your appointment is confirmed:

Date: [Day, Month Date, Year]
Time: [Time] EST
Provider: [Dr. Williams / NP Kim]
Location: 2200 Peachtree Road NE, Suite 410, Atlanta, GA 30309

Please arrive 10–15 minutes early to allow time for check-in. If you're a new patient, please complete your intake forms in advance via the Client Portal.

If you need to reschedule or cancel, please reach out at least 24 hours in advance to avoid a late-cancellation fee.

We look forward to seeing you!

Warmly,
Goodness Ajii, RN
Virtual Assistant — Sunrise Family Medicine & Wellness
hello@sunrisefamilymed.com | (404) 555-0190
```

**Tone notes:** Warm, brief, action-oriented.

**HIPAA notes:** Subject line contains no diagnosis, treatment, or specific clinical reason. Date/time are acceptable per most BAA standards.

Warmly,
Goodness Ajii, RN

**Tone notes:** Helpful, prep-focused, no anxiety-inducing language. The numbered list makes the action items scannable.

**HIPAA notes:** No PHI beyond appointment confirmation, which most patients consent to in their intake forms.

## Template 3 — Test Results Delivery (HIPAA-Compliant)

**Use case:** Notifying patient that lab/test results are ready. **Never include the actual results in email** — direct them to the secure portal.

**Subject:** A new message is waiting for you in your patient portal

**Body:**
```
Hello [First Name],

A new message from Sunrise Family Medicine & Wellness is waiting for you in your secure patient portal.

To view it, please log in here:
[Patient Portal Link]

If you've forgotten your portal password, use the "Forgot password" option on the login screen, or reply to this email and I'll help you reset it.

If you don't have portal access yet and would like to set it up, please reply and I'll send you an invitation.

For your security, we never send health information directly through email.

Warmly,
Goodness Ajii, RN
Virtual Assistant — Sunrise Family Medicine & Wellness
hello@sunrisefamilymed.com | (404) 555-0190
```

**Tone notes:** Reassuring, security-forward, no implied urgency unless the provider has flagged the result as urgent (in which case the provider — not the VA — calls directly).

**HIPAA notes:** This is the **gold standard** for results communication. The email tells the patient something is waiting without disclosing what. All clinical content stays in the portal where it's encrypted and access-logged.

Option 2: [Day, Date, Time]
Option 3: [Day, Date, Time]

Just reply with your preferred option, or click here to book directly: [Booking Link]

If none of these work, please share a few times that fit your schedule and I'll find something that works.

Talk soon!

Warmly,
Goodness Ajii, RN
Virtual Assistant — Sunrise Family Medicine & Wellness
hello@sunrisefamilymed.com | (404) 555-0190

**Tone notes:** Caring but not pushy. Offers easy reply paths (reply with option, click link, suggest your own).

## Template 5 — Prescription Refill Response

**Use case:** Replying to a patient's request for a prescription refill.

**Subject:** Re: Your medication refill request

**Body:**
```
Hello [First Name],

Thank you for reaching out about your refill request. I've forwarded your message to [Provider Name] for review.

Here's what to expect next:

- Most refills are processed within 1–2 business days.
- You'll receive a separate notification when the prescription has been sent to your pharmacy.
- If [Provider Name] needs to discuss your medication before refilling, you'll hear from us within 24 hours.

If you need this medication urgently — for example, you've run out today or have a serious health concern — please call us directly at (404) 555-0190 so we can prioritize.

Thank you for your patience!

Warmly,
Goodness Ajii, RN
Virtual Assistant — Sunrise Family Medicine & Wellness
hello@sunrisefamilymed.com | (404) 555-0190
```

**Tone notes:** Sets clear expectations, offers an urgency path. Patients HATE black-box waiting — this template removes the uncertainty.

**HIPAA notes:** No medication name in subject or body. Patient already knows what they requested.

## Template 6 — Insurance Verification Request

**Use case:** Reaching out to a patient for missing or updated insurance information before their visit.

**Subject:** Quick request before your upcoming visit

**Body:**
```
Hello [First Name],

I'm reaching out because we need to verify your insurance information before your upcoming visit with Sunrise Family Medicine on [Date].

Could you reply to this email with the following:

1. Insurance carrier name (e.g., "Blue Cross PPO")
2. Member ID number
3. Group number (if applicable)
4. Subscriber name and date of birth (if you're not the primary subscriber)

You can also upload a photo of the front and back of your insurance card directly into the patient portal here: [Portal Link]

Doing this in advance ensures a smooth check-in and prevents any billing surprises.

Thank you so much!

Warmly,
Goodness Ajii, RN
Virtual Assistant — Sunrise Family Medicine & Wellness
hello@sunrisefamilymed.com | (404) 555-0190
```

**Tone notes:** Practical, numbered, with two response options (reply with info or upload to portal).

**HIPAA notes:** Insurance information is PHI, but the patient is providing it themselves. The portal upload is the more secure path; replies in email are acceptable but never proactive.

## Template 7 — No-Show Follow-Up

**Use case:** A patient missed their appointment and didn't call ahead.

**Subject:** We missed you today — let's reschedule

**Body:**
```
Hello [First Name],

We had you scheduled with [Provider Name] today at [Time], and we missed seeing you. I hope everything is okay.

If something came up, no problem — let's get you on the calendar for a new time. Here are a few options:

Option 1: [Day, Date, Time]
Option 2: [Day, Date, Time]
Option 3: [Day, Date, Time]

Please be aware that our practice has a [$50] no-show fee, which has been added to your account. If today's missed visit was due to an emergency or illness, please let us know — we're happy to review.

Reply to this email or give us a call at (404) 555-0190 to rebook.

We look forward to seeing you soon.

Warmly,
Goodness Ajii, RN
Virtual Assistant — Sunrise Family Medicine & Wellness
hello@sunrisefamilymed.com | (404) 555-0190
```

**Tone notes:** Warm but firm. Names the policy without lecturing. Always offers an out for genuine emergencies. Patients respect honesty about fees more than passive-aggressive surprise charges.

**HIPAA notes:** "Appointment with [Provider Name]" is acceptable per most BAAs. No clinical reason mentioned.

## Template 8 — General Patient Question Response

**Use case:** Patient sent a question that doesn't fit a specific template — could be about hours, address, services, etc.

**Subject:** Re: Your question for Sunrise Family Medicine

**Body:**
```
Hello [First Name],

Thank you for reaching out — I'm happy to help.

[Direct, plain-English answer to their question. Keep it to 1–3 sentences.]

[If clinically relevant or beyond scope:]
For specific clinical questions about [topic], I'll need to forward your message to [Provider Name]. You'll hear back within 24 business hours.

[If they need to take action:]
The next step would be: [clear action with link or phone number].

If you have any other questions, just reply — I'm here to help.

Warmly,
Goodness Ajii, RN
Virtual Assistant — Sunrise Family Medicine & Wellness
hello@sunrisefamilymed.com | (404) 555-0190
```

**Tone notes:** This is the catch-all. Use direct answers, not phone-tree phrasing. The "1–3 sentence" rule keeps replies fast and scannable.

**HIPAA notes:** Anything clinical gets escalated to the provider. The VA never gives medical advice — even simple-sounding questions ("Should I stop taking my medication if I have a fever?") go through the provider.

## What I Avoid in Every Email

| Don't Say | Say Instead |
|---|---|
| "We can't give you results over email" *(sounds restrictive)* | "For your security, we deliver results through your secure patient portal" |
| "Your test came back positive for…" | "A new message is waiting in your portal" |
| "Doctor said…" *(too casual)* | "[Provider Name] reviewed your message and recommends…" |
| "Sorry for the delay" *(unprompted apology)* | "Thank you for your patience" |
| "ASAP" / "URGENT" / all caps | Specific timeframe ("within 24 hours") |
| "No problem" *(dismissive)* | "Of course — happy to help" |
| Closing with just "Thanks" | Full signature with name, role, practice |

## How to Use This Library in Practice

1. **Save in the practice's email tool** (Gmail templates, SimplePractice macros, or a Notion library)
2. **Personalize** the merge fields ([First Name], [Provider Name], etc.) — never send a template untouched
3. **Track usage** — note which templates get used most so you can refine them
4. **Review quarterly** — patient feedback often reveals language that could be warmer or clearer
5. **Update on practice changes** — new fees, new providers, new policies trigger template updates
