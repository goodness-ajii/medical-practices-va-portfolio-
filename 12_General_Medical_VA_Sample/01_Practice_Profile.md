## 6. Patient Population Snapshot

Family medicine sees a diverse population. Understanding the typical patient helps personalize communication.

### Persona A — "Established Family"
- Multi-generational families using the practice for years
- Need: efficient scheduling, prescription refills, quick communication

### Persona B — "Chronic Disease Patient"
- Diabetes, hypertension, thyroid disease, asthma
- Need: regular appointments, lab follow-ups, medication management

### Persona C — "New Patient Establishing Care"
- Recently moved, new insurance, looking for primary care home
- Need: smooth intake, records transfer, onboarding warmth

### Persona D — "Acute Visit Patient"
- Sick today, needs same-day appointment
- Need: fast response, calm reassurance, quick triage

## 7. Brand Voice & Communication Style

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Warm, professional, calm | Rushed or robotic |
| Plain English (no jargon with patients) | Use medical jargon without explanation |
| Address by full name first ("Mrs. Johnson") | First-name basis until invited |
| Patient-first phrasing ("How can we help today?") | Practice-first phrasing ("What do you need?") |
| Acknowledge concerns before solving | Skip empathy and go straight to logistics |

**Standard email greeting:**
> "Hello [Mr./Mrs./Dr.] [Last Name], thank you for reaching out to Sunrise Family Medicine."

**Standard email sign-off:**
> "Warmly,
> Goodness Ajii, RN
> Virtual Assistant — Sunrise Family Medicine & Wellness
> hello@sunrisefamilymed.com"

## 8. Compliance & Privacy Standards

- **HIPAA-compliant home workspace** (private, locked door, headset)
- **Signed BAA** with practice on file
- **No PHI** in personal email, SMS, or non-secure tools
- **Patient identity verification** required before discussing any health info via phone (name + DOB)
- **Minimum necessary information** principle followed in every email and chart entry
- **Incident reporting** to Office Manager within 24 hours of any privacy concern
