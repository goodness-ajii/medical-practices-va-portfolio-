## The 10-Section Workflow Story

### 📁 [01_Practice_Setup](./01_Practice_Setup/)
**Configuring the foundation**

Practice profile, business hours, locations, and basic operational settings. This is the bedrock — everything else builds on top of clean practice setup.

**What I demonstrate here:**
- Setting up the practice's identifying information
- Configuring business hours that match clinic operations
- Establishing location details for accurate directions and confirmations

---

### 📁 [02_Services](./02_Services/)
**Defining what the practice offers**

Service catalog, pricing structures, duration defaults, and service-specific settings that drive scheduling logic.

**What I demonstrate here:**
- Adding services with appropriate durations
- Configuring pricing (per-unit, flat rate, package)
- Setting service-specific buffer times
- Categorizing services for easier patient-facing browsing

---

### 📁 [03_Availability](./03_Availability/)
**Managing the calendar foundation**

Provider schedules, availability windows, recurring blocks, and time-off management. The calendar is the operational heart of any practice.

**What I demonstrate here:**
- Setting provider-specific availability
- Configuring recurring weekly schedules
- Adding time blocks for breaks, admin work, and meetings
- Managing time off and holidays

---

### 📁 [04_Clients](./04_Clients/)
**Patient record management**

Creating new client profiles, updating existing records, managing intake forms, and handling Client Portal access.

**What I demonstrate here:**
- Adding a new client with complete demographic information
- Sending Client Portal invitations
- Uploading and tagging documents
- Updating client information accurately

---

### 📁 [05_Notes](./05_Notes/)
**Documentation and progress notes**

SOAP-style progress notes, treatment notes, and the documentation templates that protect both patient and practice.

**What I demonstrate here:**
- Creating progress notes using practice templates
- Adding non-clinical administrative notes
- Maintaining the chart's audit trail
- Recognizing the line between admin documentation and clinical documentation (the latter is provider-only)

---

### 📁 [06_Billing](./06_Billing/)
**Financial workflow**

Invoice generation, payment recording, refund handling, and account balance management.

**What I demonstrate here:**
- Creating an invoice
- Recording a payment
- Issuing a refund (with proper approval flow)
- Reading account balance reports

---

### 📁 [07_Communication](./07_Communication/)
**Secure patient messaging**

Built-in secure messaging, automated reminder configuration, and email/SMS template management.

**What I demonstrate here:**
- Sending a secure message to a client
- Configuring automated appointment reminders
- Setting up template messages for common scenarios
- Maintaining HIPAA-compliant communication

### 📁 [08_Booking](./08_Booking/)
**End-to-end appointment scheduling**

The full booking workflow — from initial appointment creation through confirmation and calendar placement.

**What I demonstrate here:**
- Creating a new appointment

### 📁 [09_Prescreener](./09_Prescreener/)
**Pre-treatment intake forms**

Custom intake form configuration, completed form review, and integrating prescreening data into the chart.

## Why This Workflow Order Matters

These 10 sections are intentionally ordered from **practice foundation → daily operations → mobile fluency**. This mirrors the actual onboarding sequence I'd follow with a new client:

| Phase | Sections | What I'd Do |
|---|---|---|
| **Onboarding (Days 1–3)** | 01 Practice Setup, 02 Services, 03 Availability | Confirm I understand the practice's structure |
| **Foundational mastery (Days 4–7)** | 04 Clients, 05 Notes, 06 Billing | Get fluent with patient records |
| **Daily ops (Week 2+)** | 07 Communication, 08 Booking, 09 Prescreener | Run the live operational workflow |
| **Flexibility (Ongoing)** | 10 Mobile | Stay productive away from desktop |

A practice that hires me can expect this same logical progression in our first weeks together.
